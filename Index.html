Philosophy

One fire. Three woods. No shortcuts.

We built the hearth before we built the walls. Every plate that leaves this kitchen — bread, vegetable, fish, or steak — touches that same fire, at a different distance and a different moment in its burn.

The wood changes with the season and the dish: oak for its long, even heat; olive for a lean, peppery smoke that suits vegetables and lamb; vine cuttings, saved from a neighboring vineyard's spring prune, for a fast, fragrant burn under seafood.

Nothing is fried. Nothing is microwaved. If it can't be cooked over embers, it isn't on the menu.
